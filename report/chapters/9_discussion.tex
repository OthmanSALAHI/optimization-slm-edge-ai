\chapter{Discussion}

\vspace*{0.3cm}

The benchmark shows a clear tradeoff between fine-tuning quality and efficiency.
Adam obtains the best training and validation losses, which makes it the strongest
optimizer in terms of model adaptation. However, it is slower than SGD-based
methods and shows higher inference latency in the benchmark.

\section{Optimizer Behavior}

SGD is the most efficient trained optimizer in terms of latency, throughput, and
VRAM usage. SGD with momentum improves the validation loss compared with plain
SGD while keeping a moderate computational cost. This makes it a good compromise
for constrained environments.

\vspace*{0.15cm}

Adam benefits from adaptive learning rates and provides the best loss reduction.
AdamW with clipping and scheduling does not reach the same loss as Adam, but it
offers a more balanced result between quality and efficiency. L-BFGS and
Newton-CG are less practical because they require much more computation and
memory without providing a proportional improvement in validation loss.

\section{Edge AI Interpretation}

For Edge AI, the best optimizer is not necessarily the one with the lowest loss.
The model must also respect deployment constraints such as limited memory, low
latency, and acceptable throughput. From this perspective, Adam is a strong
quality baseline, while SGD with momentum and AdamW are better compromise
candidates.

\section{Compression Implications}

The results provide a baseline for future compression. If the objective is to
maximize quality before compression, Adam is the best candidate. If the objective
is to start from an already efficient model, SGD with momentum or AdamW may be
more appropriate. Compression techniques such as quantization or pruning should
therefore be evaluated on the most promising models from this benchmark.

\section{Overall Conclusion}

No optimizer is best for every criterion. Adam is preferred for quality, SGD is
preferred for efficiency, and SGD with momentum or AdamW can provide a practical
balance. This confirms that optimizer selection must be guided by the target
deployment context.
