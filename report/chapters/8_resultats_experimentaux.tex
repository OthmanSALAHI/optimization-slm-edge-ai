\chapter{Experimental Results}

\vspace*{0.3cm}

This chapter presents the benchmark results obtained on a machine equipped with a
performant GPU. The benchmark compares the optimizers on
\texttt{google/flan-t5-small} using 10 training steps and a batch size of 4.

\section{Benchmark Setup}

The tested configurations are: inference-only baseline, SGD, SGD with momentum,
Adam, AdamW with clipping and scheduler, L-BFGS, and Newton-CG. The comparison
uses loss, latency, throughput, and memory usage.

\section{Analysis}

Adam obtains the best training and validation losses, with a final training loss
of 9.4310 and a validation loss of 9.2548. This shows that Adam is the most
effective optimizer for improving model quality in this short benchmark.

\vspace*{0.15cm}

SGD with momentum improves over plain SGD. Its validation loss is 10.5485,
compared with 10.8049 for SGD. This confirms that momentum improves the basic SGD
update while keeping the method efficient.

\vspace*{0.15cm}

From an efficiency perspective, SGD is the fastest trained optimizer. It reaches
84.97 ms per training step, 211.06 ms inference latency, and 18076.50 tokens per
second. SGD with momentum remains close, with 92.56 ms per step and 16595.08
tokens per second.

\vspace*{0.15cm}

Adam provides better quality but is slower, with 190.80 ms per step and 1246.35
ms inference latency. AdamW with clipping and scheduling gives a more balanced
result: it does not reach Adam's validation loss, but it keeps latency and
throughput closer to SGD-based methods.

\vspace*{0.15cm}

L-BFGS and Newton-CG are the least practical methods in this setup. L-BFGS uses
5281.85 MB of VRAM, while Newton-CG reaches 6334.53 MB and has the slowest step
latency at 1445.42 ms. These costs are too high for an Edge AI-oriented workflow.

\section{Conclusion}

The benchmark highlights a clear tradeoff. Adam is the best optimizer for model
quality, but SGD-based methods are more efficient for latency, throughput, and
memory usage. For future compression and Edge AI deployment, SGD with momentum or
AdamW with clipping and scheduling represent practical compromise candidates,
while Adam remains the strongest quality baseline.
